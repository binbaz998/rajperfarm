Our Story

A Living Estate Rooted in Sindh's Agricultural Soul

Shahbaz Rajper Farm spans more than 50 acres of cultivated land in Goth Khair Muhammad Mojai, Kotlalu — a landscape shaped by the rhythms of seasons, the reliability of canal water, and the care of those who have tended it for generations.

From the lush canopy of banana plantations to the amber expanse of wheat fields, the farm is a living testament to what fertile Sindhi land can yield when it is tended with patience, intention, and respect for the earth.

Chairman & Founder

Mushtaq Ahmed Rajper

Custodian of the Land

Mushtaq Ahmed Rajper has shaped Shahbaz Rajper Farm not merely as an agricultural enterprise, but as a living inheritance — a place where land is treated as a responsibility, not a resource to be consumed.

Under his stewardship, the farm has grown into a diverse and enduring estate: reliable in its water, rich in its crops, and grounded in the traditions of Sindh's agricultural identity. He believes that what a family does with its land reflects its character across generations.

His vision is simple but enduring: tend the land well, honour the water, preserve the heritage, and pass it forward.

"Our land is not only our livelihood — it is our heritage, our responsibility, and our future."

— Mushtaq Ahmed Rajper
The Plantation

Banana

Lush, dense, and water-fed — the banana plantation is among the most productive and visually defining features of the estate. Grown in the fertile canal-irrigated soil of Kotlalu, each harvest reflects the consistency of both the land and its water.

The Orchard

Mangoes

The mango orchard carries a depth and stillness that only comes with age. Mature trees, cultivated with patience and water, yield a harvest that is as much about seasonal rhythm as it is about abundance.

The Golden Field

Wheat

The wheat fields stretch wide across the estate — a visual expression of the farm's scale and the openness of Sindhi agricultural land. Seasonal, golden, and elemental: wheat anchors the farm's traditional identity.

The Bloom

Mustard

When mustard blooms, the fields turn warm and vivid — a seasonal color that transforms the landscape. A crop that speaks to the richness of the soil and the diversity of what this land is capable of producing.

The Field

Cotton

Cotton is a crop that belongs deeply to the agricultural heritage of Sindh. At Shahbaz Rajper Farm, it is grown with the same care and commitment as everything else — rooted in reliable water, patient cultivation, and land that gives generously when it is respected.

Agricultural Foundation

Built on Fertile Land and Reliable Water

What makes Shahbaz Rajper Farm exceptional is not only the diversity of its crops, but the reliability of its water. A permanent, pakka RCC canal system ensures that every crop — across every season — is supported by a water supply that does not fail.

Pakka RCC Canal Infrastructure — A permanent, reinforced concrete canal system designed for long-term, dependable agricultural use.

Year-Round Water Access — The canal supply supports multiple crops across different growing seasons without interruption.

Fertile Sindhi Soil — Combined with consistent irrigation, the land yields diverse, high-quality produce across banana, mango, wheat, mustard, sesame, grapefruit, and cotton.

Heritage & Craft

Where Agriculture
Meets Heritage

The Otaq is the cultural and social heart of Shahbaz Rajper Farm — a traditional gathering space that speaks to a way of life that values craftsmanship, community, and continuity.

Hand-crafted with care, the Otaq features furniture made in Hala, Sindh — one of Pakistan's most celebrated centres of artisan woodwork and craft. Every detail reflects a deliberate choice to honour the heritage of this land alongside its agricultural function.

Here, the farm is not only a place of productivity — it is a place of welcome, of gathering, and of tradition preserved through the hands of skilled craftspeople from Sindh's own cultural heartland.
